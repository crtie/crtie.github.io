UniInsertion: A Unified Model-Based Insertion Skill Learning via Differentiable Physics-Based Simulation
In submission, sole first author

We focus on a common category of tasks in daily life, involving the insertion of an object into a confined space, where both the object to be inserted and the container may be deformable. We propose and define a new set of tasks, including rigid object insertion into deformable objects, deformable object insertion into rigid objects, and deformable object insertion into other deformable objects. Leveraging differentiable physics simulators, we present a framework that enables robots to rapidly acquire the skills for this series of insertion tasks. Across all six new tasks, our approach outperforms classical reinforcement learning algorithms in terms of data efficiency and final performance.

Leveraging SE(3) Equivariance for Learning 3D Geometric Shape Assembly
Ruihai Wu*, Chenrui Tie*, Yushi Du, Yan Zhao, Hao Dong
ICCV 2023 poster
[paper]     [project page]    

We tackle multi-part geometrically assembly task, leveraging SE(3) equivariance and invariance, which fits the physics of the task and narrows the solution space. Thus our method performs better than previous methods in two datasets, and is intuitive to future works.
